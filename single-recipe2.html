MIRCHI KA SALAN

Mirchi ka salan is a delectable and spicy Indian curry that hails from the region of Hyderabad. This rich and flavorful dish is primarily made with green chili peppers, which are simmered in a unique blend of aromatic spices, peanuts, sesame seeds, and tamarind. The result is a tantalizing combination of heat, tanginess, and nuttiness that pairs perfectly with biryani or other rice dishes. Mirchi ka salan is a popular accompaniment in traditional Hyderabadi cuisine, adding a fiery kick to the meal while satisfying the taste buds of those who love spicy food.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

6 servings

food

instructions

step 1

Slit the green chili peppers lengthwise, removing the seeds if you prefer a milder heat. Soak them in a bowl of water for 10-15 minutes.

step 2

In a dry pan, roast the peanuts, sesame seeds, and desiccated coconut separately until they turn lightly brown. Grind them into a fine paste using a little water.

step 3

Heat oil in a deep pan or skillet. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.

step 4

Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.

step 5

Stir in minced garlic and ginger and cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

step 6

Add the peanut-sesame-coconut paste and cook for a few minutes, stirring continuously.

step 6

Add the tamarind pulp and continue to cook for a few more minutes.

step 4

Adjust the salt and spice levels according to your taste.Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

ingredients

10-12 large green chili peppers

2 tablespoons oil

1 teaspoon mustard seeds

1 teaspoon cumin seeds

1 large onion, finely chopped

2 cloves of garlic, minced

1-inch piece of ginger, minced

1 tablespoon red chili powder

Cooking oil

2 tablespoons tamarind pulp

2 cups water

Salt to taste

Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

tools

Deep Pan or Skillet

Tongs or Fork

Strainer

Serving Platter or Dish
